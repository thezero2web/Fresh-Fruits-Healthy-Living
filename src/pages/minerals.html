Essential Minerals

Discover the vital role of minerals in maintaining your body's health and function

What are Minerals?

Minerals are inorganic elements that are essential for various bodily functions. They are required in different amounts - some in large quantities (macrominerals) and others in trace amounts (microminerals). Minerals play crucial roles in bone health, muscle function, nerve transmission, and maintaining fluid balance.

Calcium

Essential for strong bones, teeth, and muscle function.

Natural Sources:

  • Dairy Products
  • Leafy Greens
  • Almonds
  • Sesame Seeds

Iron

Vital for oxygen transport and energy production.

Natural Sources:

  • Red Meat
  • Spinach
  • Legumes
  • Pumpkin Seeds

Magnesium

Supports muscle and nerve function, energy production.

Natural Sources:

  • Nuts & Seeds
  • Dark Chocolate
  • Avocados
  • Bananas

Zinc

Important for immune function and wound healing.

Natural Sources:

  • Oysters
  • Pumpkin Seeds
  • Chickpeas
  • Cashews

Potassium

Regulates blood pressure and heart function.

Natural Sources:

  • Bananas
  • Sweet Potatoes
  • Spinach
  • Avocados

Selenium

Antioxidant that supports thyroid function.

Natural Sources:

  • Brazil Nuts
  • Fish
  • Eggs
  • Brown Rice

Tips for Mineral Absorption

Pair Wisely

Combine iron-rich foods with vitamin C to enhance absorption.

Timing Matters

Some minerals are better absorbed when taken with or without food.

Balance is Key

Too much of one mineral can interfere with the absorption of others.
